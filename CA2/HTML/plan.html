SP70 Achievements


For All Ages, Singapore Polytechnic at Seventy

In celebration of Singapore Polytechnic's 70th anniversary, Singapore Polytechnic is thrilled to announce the launch of a commemorative book titled ‘For All Ages: Singapore Polytechnic at Seventy’. This beautifully crafted book is a tribute to the remarkable journey of the institution, chronicling seven decades of excellence in education and innovation. It features in-depth stories of the people who have been instrumental in shaping its legacy, from pioneering educators and visionary leaders to inspiring students and alumni. Additionally, the book captures memorable moments that have defined its history, showcasing milestones, achievements, and the vibrant culture of the community.

As part of Singapore Polytechnic’s 70th Anniversary celebrations, a vibrant music festival was held on campus. Despite the rain, the enthusiasm of thousands of attendees remained high, especially when local artists like Evan Low, Rangga Jones, and Marian Carmel performed. The festival highlighted the importance of youth mental wellness, emphasizing the connection between music and mental well-being. Through this event, Singapore Polytechnic aimed to show that caring for mental health can be a joyful and communal experience, underscoring the power of music in promoting wellness.

SP70 Charity Golf and Gala Dinner
(click on the arrows to view more images)

SP70 Charity Golf at Orchid Country Club was followed by a Gala Dinner on 12 July 2024. There were nearly 1,000 attendees including staff, students, industry partners, alumni, donors and President’s Challenge beneficiaries. The welcome remarks were given by Mr. Soh Wai Wah, Principal and CEO of Singapore Polytechnic. Over $2 million in endowed donations were raised for the Singapore Polytechnic Graduates' Guild (SPGG) Education Fund, providing over 100 annual bursaries of $2,500 each for financially challenged students. A symbolic $100,000 cheque was presented to Guest-of-Honour President Tharman for the President’s Challenge to fund social causes.

Personal Work Life
Personal and professional lives are experiencing fundamental changes. A key aspect of SP's "future-ready" plan is being globally connected. This means fostering curiosity about the world's diverse cultures, which in turn builds empathy. Such an international outlook is crucial in most fields of work, enabling a better understanding of the needs of users, consumers, markets, and audiences.

Industry Disruptions
The industry landscape is becoming increasingly disruptive due to groundbreaking developments in artificial intelligence (AI), machine learning, the Internet of Things (IoT), and robotics. To stay future-ready, Singapore Polytechnic will embrace and adapt to these technological advancements and anticipate future trends in order to safeguard against significant disruption and displacement.

Global Shifts
Climate change is a top concern globally and Singapore Polytechnic’s dedication to sustainability is best demonstrated through the Sustainable Innovation Project, part of the Common Core Curriculum. This initiative offers students a learning experience that emphasizes the importance of addressing sustainability from a holistic perspective.
